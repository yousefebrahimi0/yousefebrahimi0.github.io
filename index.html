    Discovering Entrepreneurship

    Cisco Networking Academy

    Abstract

    Novel evidence is provided on the strategic usage of owner-controlled corporations as a means of tax avoidance under dual income and progressive wealth taxation. Taxpayers with a sufficient level of income face incentives to shift income towards corporations, as the joint tax liabilities of corporate and capital income taxes fall below the level of labor income taxation. Further, wealthy individuals face incentive to shift personal assets to controlled corporations or businesses to limit their personal portfolio below the exemption threshold. We exploit linked micro-data on personal income and wealth tax returns and various other sources and leverage results from several reforms in Spain which changed these incentives across taxpayers and over time to causally identify the magnitude of this behavior. We find that shifting responses are sizeable on the wealth and income dimension, and limit significantly the progressivity of the tax system.
